Desk research on how search is shifting in 2026 and what realistic one-year moves look like for a bilingual adventure-photographer-writer. Grounded in 2026 SEO source material from Nightwatch, Frase, SearchEngineLand, Anna Bauman (photographer-specific), and SEO Profy (travel-specific). Links at the bottom.

What’s different about search in 2026

Observed

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is now a bigger lever than classic blue-link SEO. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Bing Copilot cite sources, and citation-driven traffic reportedly converts three to four times better than click traffic. The first 100–150 words of every post function as an answer snippet; structure matters more than keyword density. Original imagery has become a bigger ranking signal for travel and photography niches, with image CTR running 1.4–4.9%. Topical authority beats keyword density — 45+ related posts on a narrow subject beat sporadic viral ones. Seasonality and pre-staging matter: publish Scandinavian ice-skating content by September, not December. Entity recognition and schema (HowTo, Person, LocalBusiness) help AI engines decide who to cite.

Your current baseline: a published blog with roughly 15–20 posts on monthly-to-biweekly cadence. Two content buckets (European peak guides + Scandinavia travel). Category pages, internal linking, date-stamped articles — solid baseline. Missing: newsletter signup, visible schema markup, image sitemap, AEO-shaped intros on existing posts, affiliate disclosure framework.

Inferred

AEO compounds with your existing strengths rather than replacing them. Original imagery is already your edge; first-person-summit narrative is already your voice. What’s missing is the first-150-words discipline on older posts and schema markup that tells AI engines who they’re citing. The agentic SEO stack that wins for one person is probably Claude + Search Console via MCP + occasional NeuronWriter for pro-grade posts — skip the all-in-one autonomous agents. Frase, Semrush agents, and similar are overkill at 15 posts.

The bilingual asset is the biggest unused lever. Ninety-one Czech podcast episodes are a content library that almost no competitor can replicate. Koruna Evropy has 45 peaks documented, roughly 15 have dedicated posts — 30 long-tail SEO targets sitting in the book.

What a realistic one-year plan looks like

Not everything at once. A compounding 12-month plan: one AEO rewrite per month on existing top-traffic posts (12 posts rewritten over the year). Six new peak guides published on a pre-stage calendar aligned with search-interest peaks (Alpine content in spring, Scandinavian autumn/winter content six weeks before season). One image alt-text + caption batch pass across the whole portfolio. One schema markup pass (HowTo + Person + Article JSON-LD). One experiment in Czech-to-English transcript-to-blog pipeline using Cowork. Don’t chase every keyword; topical authority around European peaks + Scandinavia adventure is a better moat than sprayed content.

Eight concrete recipes for Cowork

Each one is a reusable skill once built. Build them in order of payoff, not in the order listed.

  1. AEO intro rewriter — feed a post, get the first 150 words rewritten to directly answer the core question with scannable formatting, without losing voice.
  2. Peak-series outline generator — given one missing peak (e.g. Grossglockner), produce a draft outline following the structure of your strongest existing post.
  3. Seasonal publishing calendar — given the 15 published posts and the 30 missing peaks, propose a month-by-month calendar that pre-stages content before its search-interest peak.
  4. Image alt-text & caption batch — feed a folder of images + context → get descriptive alt text and SEO-friendly captions out.
  5. Schema markup generator — given a post URL, output HowTo + Article + Person schema as JSON-LD ready to paste into page source or a WordPress plugin.
  6. Internal linking audit — scan a folder of posts for un-linked mentions of other peaks, gear, or trails and suggest links.
  7. AI-search citation check — weekly prompt that queries Perplexity/ChatGPT for representative terms and reports which of your posts (if any) are being cited.
  8. Czech podcast transcript → English blog outline — take a CZ transcript, produce an EN blog draft outline structured for AEO. Unlocks the 91-episode archive for the English-speaking audience.

What not to automate: don’t let an agent write full posts unsupervised (voice matters, readers can smell AI-drafted text), don’t automate publishing (Cowork drafts, you review, you publish), don’t chase every keyword.
